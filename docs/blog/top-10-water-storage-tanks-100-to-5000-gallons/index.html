Top 10 Water Storage Tanks for Homes (From 100 to 5000 Gallons)

Published on 1/8/2025

Choosing the right tank depends on volume, location, budget, and whether the water is potable. Here are 10 common options and when they shine.

Quick picks by use case

  • Emergency/portable (100–330 gal): Reconditioned IBC tote (food‑grade), small poly barrels.
  • Budget irrigation (200–1000 gal): Rotomolded polyethylene (UV‑stabilized) above‑ground tanks.
  • Aesthetic/compact: Slimline or decorative polyethylene.
  • Large above‑ground (2500–5000+ gal): Corrugated steel with liner; modular and scalable.
  • Underground, long life: Concrete or fiberglass cisterns.
  • Low‑clearance/indoor crawlspace: Bladder tanks.

Tank types (pros/cons)

  1. Polyethylene (rotomolded)
  • Pros: Affordable, many sizes, simple install; potable models available.
  • Cons: UV exposure ages plastic; needs stable pad and sun protection.
  1. Slimline/decorative poly
  • Pros: Fits tight spaces, better curb appeal.
  • Cons: Usually higher cost per gallon; limited large sizes.
  1. Corrugated steel with liner
  • Pros: Scales to very large volumes; strong; serviceable liner replacement.
  • Cons: Higher upfront cost; requires flat pad; aesthetic/HOA considerations.
  1. Fiberglass (above or underground)
  • Pros: Durable, inert; good for underground installs.
  • Cons: Cost; may require professional install.
  1. Concrete cistern (underground)
  • Pros: Long service life; buffers pH; traffic‑rated options.
  • Cons: Heavy equipment, excavation, and permitting.
  1. Bladder tank
  • Pros: Fits crawlspaces; low profile; fast to deploy.
  • Cons: Requires protective surface; puncture risk; limited very large sizes.
  1. Reconditioned IBC tote (275–330 gal)
  • Pros: Widely available; inexpensive per gallon.
  • Cons: Verify food‑grade and prior contents; add opaque cover to block light.
  1. Galvanized steel (lined vs. unlined)
  • Pros: Rugged; farmhouse aesthetic.
  • Cons: Unlined tanks not for potable storage; corrosion concerns.
  1. Modular plastic panels/cells
  • Pros: Hidden under decks/landscapes; customizable shapes.
  • Cons: Installation complexity; geotextile/liner details matter.
  1. Wooden tanks with liners
  • Pros: Unique look; replaceable liners.
  • Cons: Maintenance; not common in all regions.

Buyer’s checklist

  • Potable rating and documentation (if drinking).
  • Opaque walls, sealed lids, screened vents/overflows.
  • Pad/foundation requirements and seismic/wind loads.
  • Fittings, unions, and future service access.
  • Freeze, sun, and algae mitigation strategies.
